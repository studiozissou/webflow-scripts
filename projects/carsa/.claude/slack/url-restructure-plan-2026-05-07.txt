Hi Tomek, following on from the SEO analysis — here's a plan for the URL restructure and sell location pages:

*Context*
Carsa's buy pages sit neatly under `/used-cars/` but the sell side is scattered (`/value-car`, `/part-exchange`, no location pages) and `/used-cars-for-sale/near/` doesn't match the rest of the site.

*Problem*
No way to rank for "sell my car in [location]" queries right now. The inconsistent URL structure also splits link equity between `/used-cars-for-sale/` and `/used-cars/`.

One thing to flag: I'd recommend against the postcode-swap pages (`/sell-car/postcode/{location}`). Pages where the only difference is the place name are what Google calls "doorway pages" — they'll either refuse to index them or suppress them in rankings. Without a physical store in that area there's nothing real backing the page up. The per-store pages below are the safe version of the same idea.

*Solution*
Group all sell pages under `/sell-car/`, move `/used-cars-for-sale/near/` to `/used-cars/near/`, and create per-store sell pages from a duplicated Stores CMS.

The work:
•  301 redirects: `/used-cars-for-sale/near/*` → `/used-cars/near/*`, `/value-car` → `/sell-car/value`, `/part-exchange` → `/sell-car/part-exchange`
•  New `/sell-car` landing page
•  Duplicate the Stores CMS collection for sell pages
•  Build `/sell-car/locations/{store}` template with valuation form, process steps, sell-specific reviews, pulling store metadata from CMS
•  Cross-links between buy and sell pages for each location
•  AutoDealer JSON-LD schema per store page

*Benefits*
•  Rankable pages for "sell my car [location]" at every Carsa store
•  All buy pages under `/used-cars/`, all sell pages under `/sell-car/` — no more orphan URLs
•  Store metadata already exists in the CMS so sell pages are quick to populate

*Risks*
•  Redirect traffic dip — 301s can cause a temporary ranking wobble for moved pages, usually recovers in 2-4 weeks
•  Content depth — each sell store page needs enough unique body copy beyond the shared template to avoid thin content flags
