Overview

Project context

TSC has gone to market with its software-based Automatic Train Protection system and the current website does not match that moment. The site is out of date, both in content and technically, and it is not doing the job it needs to do. What TSC needs now is a credibility and lead generation platform that can hold its own in a safety-critical market.

Problem

  • The current website is technically outdated and the content no longer reflects what TSC offers or where the company is heading.
  • TSC has a new flagship product to take to market and no platform to communicate its value to prospects, operators, and partners.

Solution

  • Build a website in Webflow that puts the new ATP system front and centre while clearly communicating the full product range.
  • Structure content around products, customers, and services so each audience (operators, procurement, partners) finds what they need to make a decision.
  • Set up a CMS for news and insights so the marketing team can publish independently and build thought leadership over time.

What you get

  • A site that looks like it belongs to a company building safety-critical systems. Right now, it does not.
  • Product pages that help operators, procurement teams, and partners understand the iEVC, ETCS, RailOS, and Apps offerings.
  • Lead generation through clear calls to action, contact forms, and content that brings technical buyers in through search.
  • A site the marketing team can update without waiting for a developer. News, pages, content, all editable.
  • Technical SEO baked into every page so you rank where it counts.
  • Answer Engine Optimisation (AEO). This is about structuring content so AI search tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews can find and cite TSC's expertise. Most agencies are not doing this yet.

Two ways of working

Both options deliver the same result. The difference is sequencing.

Option A: Waterfall

All design is completed and signed off before any development begins.

How it works:

  1. Full site design (all 12 pages) completed in Figma.
  2. TSC reviews and signs off the complete design.
  3. Development begins and proceeds page by page.

Pros:

  • You see the entire site design before any build work starts.
  • Easier to keep visual consistency across all pages.
  • Clear handoff point between design and development.

Cons:

  • Slower overall. Development cannot start until all designs are approved.
  • Content for all pages must be ready before design begins.

Option B: Page-by-page (recommended)

Design and development run in parallel, staggered by page.

How it works:

  1. Once a page design is complete and signed off, it goes straight into development.
  2. While that page is being built, the next page is being designed.
  3. This continues until all pages are done.

Pros:

  • Faster overall. Design and development overlap.
  • Content can be delivered page by page, which gives your team more flexibility.
  • You see working pages sooner, which helps catch issues early.

Cons:

  • Requires disciplined content delivery. If a page's content is late, the pipeline stalls.
  • Design changes to earlier pages (after seeing later ones) may require rework.

Timeline

Target launch: end of July 2026.

That gives us roughly 10 weeks from kick-off. Achievable, but tight. The biggest variable is not the design or development work. It is the availability of branding and content from TSC.

What the timeline depends on:

  • Brand guidelines (logo, colours, fonts, tone of voice) delivered before or at kick-off.
  • Page content (copy, photography, video) delivered progressively, at least 2 pages per week to keep the pipeline moving.
  • Feedback turnaround of 2–3 working days per review round.

If branding and content are delayed, the launch date shifts. I would rather flag this now than promise a date I cannot control.

Page-by-page gives us the best chance of hitting end of July because development starts as soon as the first page is signed off. Waterfall would likely push launch into August or September.

Process

In Waterfall, the steps below happen sequentially for all pages. In Page-by-page, steps 1 and 2 happen once upfront, then steps 3–5 repeat per page, so while one page is in development, the next is being designed.

  1. Branding and UI direction. I design 3 options based on brand guidelines. TSC picks one and gives feedback.
  2. Wireframes. Desktop wireframes of all pages. One round of feedback.
  3. Desktop and mobile design. Full design for the page(s). Feedback gets factored into development.
  4. Desktop build. I build the page(s) and provide a test version for review.
  5. Responsive build. Responsive version for review. Final changes made here.
  6. De-snagging. Last corrections, plus training and documentation on how to use the site.
  7. Site launch and handover to TSC's Webflow account.
