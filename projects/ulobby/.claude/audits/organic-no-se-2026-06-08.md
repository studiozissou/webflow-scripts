## 1. Executive Summary

Norway and Sweden are effectively dormant markets for Ulobby. The numbers are stark:

- **Norway:** 2 organic keywords, 0 estimated traffic, domain rank ~2.2M
- **Sweden:** 2 organic keywords, 0 estimated traffic, domain rank ~2.8M

For comparison, Ulobby has 15 keywords in Denmark and 70 in the US. These Scandinavian markets are not just weak -- they are functionally invisible to search engines.

However, the opportunity is real. Norwegian searches for "lobbyvirksomhet" (720/mo), "lobbying" (1,900/mo), and "lobbyregister" (320/mo) show genuine demand for lobbying/public affairs content. Sweden has even larger volumes: "lobbying" (1,300/mo), "lobbyist" (1,300/mo), "lobbyism" (1,300/mo), and "opinionsbildning" (480/mo). No SaaS competitor ranks for any of these terms in either market. The SERP leaders are encyclopedias (SNL, Wikipedia), government sites (Stortinget, Riksdagen), and a single Swedish PR agency (Influera.com).

**The core problem:** Ulobby's /no/ and /sv/ locale pages exist but contain thin, untranslated, or insufficiently localised content. Google is not indexing them for local-language queries. The only rankings come from English-language blog content that happens to match English-borrowed terms ("public affairs definition" in NO, "global public affairs" in SE).

## 10. Analysis: Why These Markets Are Dormant

### Root Causes

**1. No local-language content strategy.**
The /no/ and /sv/ locale pages appear to be direct translations or thin adaptations of English content. They are not targeting Norwegian or Swedish search terms. The only keywords Ulobby ranks for in both markets are English-language terms that happen to match -- not deliberate targeting.

**2. Hreflang / locale indexing issues.**
Sweden showed a complete dropout in Jan 2026 (0 keywords, no domain rank). This suggests possible technical issues with how the /sv/ locale is being crawled and indexed. The brief traffic spike in Nov-Dec 2025 (32 visits) coincided with AdWords activity, which may have temporarily boosted organic indexing -- but it did not stick.

**3. English content does not transfer.**
Google.no and Google.se increasingly prefer local-language results. PAN's collapse in both markets (NO: 18 keywords to 0; SE: 24 keywords to 11, traffic 46 to 1) proves that English content is losing ground in Scandinavian SERPs. Ulobby's English blog posts cannot carry these markets.

**4. No topical authority in either language.**
Ulobby has zero Norwegian-language pages ranking for Norwegian terms and zero Swedish-language pages ranking for Swedish terms. Without a cluster of related content in each language, Google has no reason to consider Ulobby an authority on public affairs in these markets.

**5. The /no/ and /sv/ pages lack search-targeted content.**
Product/solution pages (like /sv/solutions/reporting-and-oversight ranking for "oversight svenska") are not designed to capture informational search intent. The market needs definitional, educational content ("Hva er lobbyvirksomhet?", "Vad betyder public affairs?") to build the top of funnel.
