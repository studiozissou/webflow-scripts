Hi Romain,

I've been through the latest content document and I want to propose a cleaner nav before I build it out. The current sitemap has ~18 pages and Jarlath mapped 6 top-level menu items — I think we can do it in 5 plus the Contact button, which makes the site easier to scan and keeps the focus on what matters commercially.

Here's the full layout:

**Header Nav**

- **RailOS** (dropdown)
  - RailOS Overview
  - RailOS Apps
  - RailOS Devices
  - Open RailOS
  - RailOS App Store
- **Products**
- **Services**
- **Projects**
- **Company** (dropdown)
  - About Us
  - Leadership
  - Careers
  - Insights & News
- **[Contact Us]** (button)

RailOS gets top billing because it's the differentiator and should feel like its own ecosystem. Products, Services, and Projects are single pages (no dropdowns, fast to reach). Company groups the trust-building pages together and frees up a nav slot. Insights is still one click away but doesn't take up prime header space. Contact Us is styled as a button, always visible, which is standard for the primary conversion action.

Projects replaces "Customers" because the case studies are the most impactful and relevant content.

**Footer**

The footer mirrors the nav but goes deeper — every page and sub-section gets a link:

- **RailOS** — Overview, Apps, Devices, Open RailOS, App Store
- **Products** — ETCS, TOBA Box, PZB, KVB, TBL1+, wSTM (anchor links to each product section)
- **Services** — Retrofit Assessment, Homologation, Installation & Commissioning, Training, Maintenance (anchor links)
- **Company** — About Us, Leadership, Careers, Insights & News, FAQ, Contact
- **Projects** — Lineas HLD77, Skoda / RegioJet, Akiem BR189 (listed by name, not a generic "View projects" link)

Bottom bar: copyright, address, BCE number, legal pages.

Everything from the content doc should be in there.

Two quick questions:

1. **"Projects" vs "Our Projects"** — I saw some discussion about using "Our Projects" but plain "Projects" is cleaner for the top level nav.
2. **Insights under Company** — are you comfortable with it being here or do you want it at the top level? It's easy to promote later if the blog grows.

Happy to walk through it on tomorrow's call if easier.
