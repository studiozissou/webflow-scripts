Hi Romain,

Following on from our chat about using Claude to help with the site's SEO, here's a small example so you can see the quality before we roll it out more widely. I picked two pages — the RailOS product page and one news article — scanned the live copy on staging, and drafted a search title and meta description for each.

Quick reminder on what these two things are: the **title** is the clickable blue headline in Google results, and the **description** is the grey summary line underneath. Both are what convince someone to click, so they're worth getting right.

---

**1. RailOS page**
Staging: https://tsc-v2.webflow.io/railos

- **Title:** RailOS: Software-Defined ATP Platform for ETCS & Class B
- **Description:** RailOS runs ETCS and Class B systems (TBL1+, PZB, KVB) on one SIL4-certified platform — upgradable by software, not hardware. Explore the platform.

*Why:* It leads with the product name for anyone already searching "RailOS", then front-loads the phrase buyers actually type ("software-defined ATP platform"). The description names the specific standards you support so a technical reader immediately sees relevance, and it ends on the core differentiator — software-upgradable, no hardware swap.

---

**2. News article**
Staging: https://tsc-v2.webflow.io/news/worlds-first-software-defined-etcs-certified

- **Title:** World's First Software-Defined ETCS Safety System Certified
- **Description:** TÜV Rheinland has certified TSC's on-board ETCS as the world's first software-defined train protection system — now adopted by Škoda Group.

*Why:* It leans on the strongest hook you have ("world's first… certified"), and the description packs the two credibility signals — the certifier (TÜV Rheinland) and the marquee adopter (Škoda Group) — into the snippet, because on a safety-critical brand those names do the persuading.

---

A couple of principles I'm applying throughout, in case useful:

- **Length:** titles stay under ~60 characters and descriptions under ~155, so Google doesn't cut them off with "…" in the results.
- **No invented claims:** every line is drawn from copy already on the page. Nothing is embellished, which matters for a company in your field.
- **Consistency:** where there's room I'd append a standard "| The Signalling Company" to titles so the brand shows in every result.
