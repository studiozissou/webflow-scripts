## ICP 1: Hospitality Employer (B2B — primary revenue driver)

### Profile
- **Title:** HR Director, VP People, General Manager, COO, or Owner
- **Company type:** Hotels, resorts, cruise lines, casinos, F&B groups, luxury brands
- **Company size:** 50-5,000+ employees (mid-market to enterprise)
- **Geography:** Europe primarily (NL, UK, FR, DE, IT, ES), expanding globally
- **Segments served:** 17 hospitality industry segments (per site)
- **Budget:** Retained search fees or contingency placement fees

### Pain points
- Hard-to-fill senior/specialist roles (GM, Executive Chef, Revenue Director)
- High cost of bad hires in hospitality (turnover, guest experience impact)
- Need for discretion when replacing incumbent leaders
- Time pressure — seasonal hiring cycles, pre-opening teams
- Difficulty finding talent across borders / language barriers

### Decision triggers
- New hotel/restaurant opening requiring a full leadership team
- Underperforming property needing new GM or department heads
- Expansion into new market/geography
- Confidential replacement of current executive
- Board-mandated talent upgrade

### What they value
- Speed to shortlist (weeks, not months)
- Quality of candidates (pre-vetted, culturally aligned)
- Industry expertise (recruiter who understands hospitality operations)
- Discretion (especially for confidential searches)
- Long-term relationship (not transactional, repeat business)

### Content that attracts them
- Case studies: "How we placed a GM for a 5-star resort in 3 weeks"
- Salary benchmarks / market intelligence by role and region
- Industry trend reports (staffing challenges, compensation trends)
- Process transparency: "What to expect when you engage us"
- Testimonials from peer HR leaders

## ICP 2: Hospitality Professional / Candidate (B2C — volume driver)

### Profile
- **Career stage:** Mid-career to senior (5-20+ years experience)
- **Current role:** Department head, GM, Executive Chef, Revenue Manager, F&B Director
- **Aspiration:** Next-level role, international move, career pivot within hospitality
- **Geography:** Europe-based, open to international relocation
- **Segments:** Hotels, resorts, cruise, F&B, luxury retail/travel

### Sub-segments
| Level | Description | Site reference |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Junior / Trainee | Hotelschool graduates, first 1-3 years | /hotelschools, /levels |
| Mid-level | 3-10 years, department supervisors/managers | /for-candidates |
| Senior / C-level | 10+ years, GMs, VPs, Directors | /for-candidates, /personal-career-management |

### Pain points
- Stuck in current role, limited internal progression
- Want international exposure but don't know how to access it
- Applying through job boards feels impersonal and unproductive
- Concerned about confidentiality (current employer must not know)
- Unsure of market value / what roles they qualify for

### Decision triggers
- Passed over for promotion
- Hotel ownership change / management company switch
- Contract ending (especially in Middle East / Asia)
- Life change requiring relocation
- Approached by recruiter (passive candidate activation)

### What they value
- Access to unadvertised roles ("exclusive opportunities")
- Personalised career advice (not just job matching)
- Confidentiality (especially if currently employed)
- Industry-specific expertise (recruiter who "gets" hospitality)
- Long-term career partnership (not one-off placement)

### Content that attracts them
- Job listings with real detail (salary range, property type, team size)
- Career advice articles: "How to transition from Operations to Commercial"
- Salary guides by role, region, and property type
- Interview preparation guides specific to hospitality
- Success stories: "From Sous Chef to Executive Chef in 18 months"

## ICP 3: UHNW / Royal / Family Office (B2C — highest value per engagement)

### Profile
- **Title:** Principal, Family Office Manager, Chief of Staff, Estate Manager
- **Household type:** Royal household, UHNW family, family office, private estate
- **Staff needs:** Estate managers, executive housekeepers, private chefs, butlers, close protection, household PAs, nannies, yacht crew
- **Geography:** Global (Europe, Middle East, Asia, Americas)
- **Budget:** Premium — price is secondary to quality and discretion

### Pain points
- Finding staff who understand ultra-high-net-worth household standards
- Background vetting and security clearance requirements
- Cultural fit is critical — staff live in or near the household
- Previous agencies sent inappropriate or unvetted candidates
- NDA and confidentiality management
- High turnover due to poor cultural matching

### Decision triggers
- New property acquisition (estate, yacht, chalet)
- Staff departure (often sudden — needs fast replacement)
- Scaling household (growing family, expanding properties)
- Upgrading from informal hiring to professional recruitment
- Referral from another UHNW family or family office

### What they value
- Absolute discretion (NDA-first approach)
- Deep vetting (background, references, security, cultural fit)
- Understanding of UHNW household dynamics
- Global reach with local knowledge
- Single point of contact (not a team of juniors)
- Speed without compromising quality

### Content that attracts them
- NOT blog posts or public content (this audience doesn't search Google for staff)
- Referral-driven — word of mouth, family office networks, wealth management introductions
- Minimal public-facing content; the /for-royals page serves as a credibility gate
- Private consultation process (contact form → personal call → NDA → brief)
- Discrete case references (anonymised): "Staffed a 12-person household for a European royal family"
