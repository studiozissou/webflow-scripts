## Positioning Shift

**From:** Portfolio site aimed at agencies — "careful web design studio"
**To:** Retainer-focused service site — "website valet"

### Core Concept: Website Valet

One person. Deep expertise. Fast hands. You hand something valuable to someone
you trust — they handle it with care, you get it back better than you left it.

The valet metaphor works because it's personal (not institutional), premium
(not service-class), and implies ongoing custody — not a one-off project.

**Tagline:** `Studio Zissou — website valet.`

### Three Pillars
1. **Design & Build** — Webflow sites, interfaces, interactive experiences (existing strength)
2. **Automate & Integrate** — AI workflows, content pipelines, CMS automation (new direction)
3. **Manage & Grow** — SEO/AEO, performance monitoring, ongoing site ops (retainer play)

Webflow becomes one tool in the kit, not the whole offering.

### Key Insight (implicit, never stated explicitly)

Many clients get their sites built by big fancy agencies, then get handed to a
junior PM for aftercare. They want a senior contact with deep expertise who can
execute fast. That's the pain — but the copy should *show* what we offer, not
*name* the problem. Let the reader fill in the contrast.

---

## Primary Audience: End Clients (Retainer Target)

- **Pain:** Handed to a junior PM after agency build; website neglected, losing visibility
- **Sell:** One senior person, ongoing attention, clear monthly rhythm, compounding results
- **Proof:** Duration of client relationships, metrics, design quality
- **Profile:** Mid-market+, existing site built by known agency, frustrated with aftercare, values craft

## Secondary Audience: Agencies

- Served via dedicated strip, not as primary message
- Pain: Need reliable design/build partner
- Sell: Taste + reliability + low-maintenance embedded work

---

## Brand Voice

- Short, unpretentious, slightly dry
- Confident on technical depth, warm on relationship
- Avoid "values" vocabulary (care, respect, quality) unless paired with concrete outcomes
- Strongest existing line: "Performance isn't bolted on at the end" — write everything in this register

**Tension to preserve:** Technically sharp but gives a shit about how things feel. Do not resolve — lean into it.

## Charm Strategy

Charm is the differentiator vs every "AI automation agency". Preserve it via:

- **Visual system (~70%)** — hand-drawn typography, Ghibli/OP-1/geoglyph illustrations, ligne claire submarine decorations
- **Copy voice (~30%)** — "hand me the keys", "keep earning their keep", "send it over", "three things, done well"

Technical sections: confident, plain. Soft sections (Hero, Contact): warmth. The contrast *is* the brand.

---

## Valet Metaphor — Usage Notes

Use "valet" in the hero/descriptor. Let it echo lightly elsewhere — don't force it
into every section. Specific echoes:

| Section | Valet echo |
|---------|-----------|
| What I Do | "What the valet does while you're away" framing |
| How It Works | "While your site is parked with me" |
| Work | "Clients who've left their keys with me" |
| Contact | "Hand over the keys →" as CTA |

**Adjacent metaphors worth exploring:** Keeper (Ghibli warmth), Steward (senior trust).
Valet is sharpest for premium/personal. Keeper is best for Ghibli/craft. Don't need
to pick one — they can coexist as voice registers.
