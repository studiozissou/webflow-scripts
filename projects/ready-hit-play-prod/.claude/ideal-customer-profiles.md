# Ready Hit Play — Ideal Customer Profiles

## ICP 1: The Brand Director at a Scaling Tech Company

**Name:** "Strategic Sarah"
**Role:** VP Brand / Head of Marketing at a Series B–D tech company (50–500 employees)
**Market:** US or Netherlands

**Goals:**
- Elevate brand from startup scrappiness to category-defining presence
- Create a flagship brand film or campaign for a product launch, funding round, or IPO
- Find a creative partner who understands ambition, not just deliverables

**Pain Points:**
- Internal creative teams are stretched thin and output feels safe
- Previous agencies delivered polished work that didn't move anyone
- Needs to justify creative spend to a board that thinks in metrics

**Language they use:**
- "We need something that actually lands"
- "How do we stand out without shouting?"
- "We're past the startup phase — the brand needs to catch up"

**What they need from the site:**
- Proof of emotional impact (not just visual polish)
- Evidence RHP works at their scale and ambition level
- A fast path to conversation — no lengthy intake forms

## ICP 2: The Creative Lead at a Fashion/Luxury Multinational

**Name:** "Decisional David"
**Role:** Creative Director or Global Marketing Lead at a fashion house or luxury brand
**Market:** Netherlands/EU headquarters, global audience

**Goals:**
- Produce brand films and digital experiences for seasonal campaigns or brand repositioning
- Partner with a studio that speaks the language of taste, not templates
- Maintain brand integrity while pushing into new formats (web, film, motion)

**Pain Points:**
- Most digital agencies don't understand craft or restraint
- Tired of decks and processes — wants creative instinct backed by strategy
- Needs a partner who can move fast for campaign timelines

**Language they use:**
- "We don't need more content. We need fewer, better pieces."
- "The story has to feel inevitable, not manufactured"
- "Show me you understand the world we operate in"

**What they need from the site:**
- Cinematic quality in the work showcase — the site itself is the proof
- Sparse, confident copy that signals shared aesthetic values
- No bloated case studies — just the work and the impact

## ICP 3: The Founder Who Needs to Be Believed

**Name:** "Pivotal Pete"
**Role:** Founder/CEO at a company facing a defining moment — launch, rebrand, fundraise, or acquisition
**Market:** US or Netherlands

**Goals:**
- Craft a narrative that makes investors, customers, or acquirers feel the vision
- Translate complex or abstract value into something people feel in 60 seconds
- Get messaging and film assets that work across pitch rooms and public stages

**Pain Points:**
- Knows the company story is powerful but can't articulate it compellingly
- Previous branding work felt generic — "could have been any company"
- The stakes are high and the window is short

**Language they use:**
- "People need to feel what we're building"
- "We have 5 minutes to make them believe"
- "I don't want a brand guide. I want a weapon."

**What they need from the site:**
- Immediate sense that RHP understands high-stakes moments
- Copy that mirrors how they want to sound — commanding, clear, undeniable
- "It starts with a conversation" — low-friction entry point, personal tone
