# Design State -- Carsa

> Current visual identity and design patterns as they exist on the live site.
> Last reviewed: 2026-04-23

## Visual identity

**Aesthetic:** Clean, modern automotive. The site prioritises function over
decoration -- every visual element serves the goal of helping someone find and
enquire about a car.

**Colour palette:**
- Dark backgrounds (near-black) for primary sections
- Yellow accent for CTAs and highlights (Carsa brand yellow)
- White/light backgrounds for content-heavy sections (search results, finance info)
- Neutral greys for secondary text and borders

**Typography:** Sans-serif throughout. Clear hierarchy between headings and body
text. Headings are bold and direct. Body text is readable at all breakpoints.

## Layout patterns

- **Card-based vehicle listings** on search results and collection pages
- **Section-based page structure** following Client First (Section > padding > container > content)
- **Two-column layouts** for feature/benefit sections (text + image)
- **Full-width hero sections** with search functionality on the homepage
- **Grid layouts** for store locations, blog posts, and model browsing

## Photography

- Professional vehicle photography is central to the experience
- Multiple angles per vehicle on detail pages
- Clean, consistent backgrounds in stock photography
- Store/location photography for trust-building

## Animation and motion

- Minimal animation. Functional over decorative.
- No parallax scrolling or complex scroll-triggered effects
- Subtle hover states on interactive elements (buttons, cards)
- Page transitions are instant (no Barba.js or GSAP page transitions)

## What Carsa never does

- Flashing banners or countdown timers
- Pop-ups interrupting the browsing experience
- Auto-playing video or audio
- Decorative animation that slows page load
- Dark patterns (hidden fees, pre-checked options, misleading CTAs)
