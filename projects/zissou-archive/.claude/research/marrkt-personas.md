## Key Themes Across All Profiles

1. **"Rolling wardrobe" is the dominant model** — these sellers rotate winners to fund the next chapter, not declutter failures
2. **Pre-worn = pre-softened** — for workwear, denim, OCBDs, age is a genuine value-add
3. **Presentation is the product** — Marrkt's entire edge is steaming, styling, and shooting every item to retail standard
4. **Context and narrative sell** — every piece has an original function and a new life; telling both stories turns a listing into editorial
5. **Repro-becomes-vintage pipeline** — Visvim, Real McCoy's, Iron Heart from the 2000s are legitimate vintage now
6. **Deadstock from indie designers** — archive access is a distinct, highly covetable category
7. **Community before commerce** — the most durable sellers are those whose identity people buy into
8. **Country-of-manufacture matters** — "Made in USA" North Face or "Made in France" Moncler commands different attention than current production

## Profiles

### Lewis Hull — Marrkt Founder
**Profession:** Founder of MARRKT. Started in menswear 2006. Previously ran York's first boutique hotel. Founded Superdenim in 2007 (Japanese denim specialist). Early relationships with The Real McCoy's, Viberg, Orslow — documented them for Inventory Magazine. MARRKT launched as deadstock site 2014, pivoted to managed pre-owned marketplace 2018/2019.
**Style:** Quality-first, provenance-conscious. UK, North American, and European manufacture.
**Key brands:** New Balance Made in USA, Arc'teryx/Veilance, Drake's, Margaret Howell, Edward Green, Thom Sweeney.
**Quotes:** "We've essentially taken what are 'second hand goods' and presented them in a shopping environment similar to Mr Porter, Clutch Cafe or The Bureau Belfast." / "I felt the quality of products we'd been selling over the years was so high it was designed to last — perfect for resale and it would retain value."
**Insight:** The presentation gap is the business. Photography and presentation standards are the single biggest competitive lever.

### Simon Crompton — Permanent Style
**Profession:** Founder of Permanent Style. Author. One of the most respected menswear voices.
**Style:** Evolved from slim tailored cuts toward comfort and larger fitting. Appreciates vintage with character.
**Key brands:** Gucci, Ralph Lauren, Eddie Bauer, vintage labels.
**Quotes:** "I like buying an old leather jacket that's been beaten in and shaped by someone else for a few years, and then taking it on myself and adding my part in its story."
**Insight:** Narrative is the most compelling thing you can offer a buyer. A garment with character and history is more interesting than the same piece new.

### Guy Berryman — Coldplay / Applied Art Forms
**Profession:** Bassist in Coldplay. Founder of menswear label Applied Art Forms. Editor of car magazine Road Rat. Collector of archival menswear, watches, and mid-century furniture.
**Style:** Oversized, boxy silhouettes from vintage military/utilitarian references. Influenced by 90s Margiela and Helmut Lang.
**Key brands:** Applied Art Forms (own), Helmut Lang, Jil Sander, Raf Simons, Rick Owens, Visvim, Comme des Garcons, Yohji Yamamoto.
**Quotes:** "I design almost everything we make to look better as it ages, fades and takes on character."
**Insight:** Early pieces by Raf Simons or Rick Owens appreciate beyond original retail — provenance and era matter enormously.

### Adam Goldberg — Actor
**Profession:** Actor (Saving Private Ryan, Friends, Dazed & Confused). Musician. Self-styles his characters' wardrobes.
**Style:** Vintage US and Japanese repro menswear, 1950s-70s aesthetics. Prefers repro over original vintage: "you get to make it your own."
**Key brands:** The Real McCoy's, Kapital, RRL, Visvim, Left Field NYC, Indigofera, Mister Freedom, Iron Heart, Freenote Cloth, Clinch/Brass Tokyo.
**Quotes:** "I buy too much." Notes the irony that repro pieces will themselves become vintage.
**Insight:** The repro-to-vintage pipeline is real. Pieces bought new from Real McCoy's or Iron Heart in 2005 are now legitimately aged artifacts.

### Geordie D'Anyers Willis — Berry Bros. & Rudd
**Profession:** Creative Director at Britain's oldest wine and spirits merchant (est. 1698). Eighth-generation family member.
**Style:** Heritage menswear, "buy less, buy better." Moved away from recognizable labels toward specialist selections.
**Key brands:** Drake's, Church's, heritage British menswear.
**Quotes:** "Whatever we sell this time, all that money will go back into buying something nice new." / "Customers become friends. Friends become customers."
**Insight:** The buy-less-buy-better customer is motivated by active wardrobe curation, not decluttering guilt. Frame selling as reinvesting in quality.

### Steve Sanderson — Oi Polloi / Karévan
**Profession:** Co-founded Oi Polloi (Manchester). Now co-runs Karévan — design studio, showroom, and publishing platform.
**Style:** Curatorial and contextual. Functional pieces given new meaning through placement.
**Key brands:** Sanders, FPM Milano, Lyle & Scott (Double Cashmere). Previously championed Birkenstock, Berghaus before mainstream.
**Quotes:** "We'd go around finding things people had never heard of and put them into a different context."
**Insight:** Context and narrative sell clothes. An item from a niche brand becomes desirable when you explain its original purpose and reframe it.

### Tom Hoy — Easy On The Ivy / Rivet & Hide
**Profession:** Menswear writer. Runs Substack "Easy On The Ivy." Works at Rivet & Hide (Manchester). Previously Pretty Green.
**Style:** Deep Ivy League / Amekaji enthusiast. Collects vintage Popeye Magazine. Does chain stitch hemming on Union Special machines.
**Key brands:** Brooks Brothers, heritage Ivy labels, Japanese Amekaji brands.
**Quotes:** "I am a true believer that clothes look best when they have been lived in. They should get better over time."
**Insight:** For garments that genuinely improve with age (OCBD shirts, raw denim, leather), lead with the fact that previous wear is a feature, not a flaw.

### Justin Hast — Watch Journalist
**Profession:** Watch journalist. Contributor to Hodinkee, Mr Porter, The Rake. YouTube channel. Authoring books on independent watchmakers.
**Style:** Refined casualness. Navy twill trousers, white tees, suede bombers, quality sneakers.
**Key brands:** Drake's, Rocky Mountain Featherbed, Anglo Italian, Orslow, Alden, Rubinacci, Warehouse Japan.
**Quotes:** "If it isn't comfortable — it just doesn't get worn, and there is nothing I hate more than owning something that isn't used."
**Insight:** Wearability is a powerful editorial filter. Comfort and versatility are undersold virtues in premium menswear.

### James Brown — Loaded Magazine Creator
**Profession:** Journalist. Creator of Loaded magazine. Author.
**Style:** Japanese fashion enthusiast (particularly Kapital). Frequents vintage shops in Tokyo.
**Key brands:** Kapital (specifically embroidered satin jackets).
**Insight:** Cultural creatives — editors, writers, musicians — are a core seller demographic. Their identity lends authority to pieces they sell. Don't undersell the provenance of who owned it.

### Daniel Jenkins — Purposeful Activity
**Profession:** Creator of independent menswear publication Purposeful Activity. 20+ years in the industry. Writes and photographs using a Leica Q2.
**Style:** Craftsmanship, longevity, daily use as quality measures. Anti-trend, anti-seasonal.
**Key brands:** RRL, The Real McCoy's, Edward Green, Visvim, Rocky Mountain Featherbed.
**Quotes:** "Buy once, buy better, live with beauty and use well." Owns one McCoy's sweatshirt worn nearly every day for 8.5 years.
**Insight:** The 8.5-year sweatshirt is the killer proof point — if a garment survives that use and still has value, resale is natural. Longevity is the argument for both buying quality new and buying quality pre-owned.

### Jamie Ferguson — Menswear Photographer
**Profession:** Menswear photographer and author. This Thing Of Ours. Author of "This Guy: Portraits of Modern Men's Style." Co-authoring a casual menswear book with Simon Crompton.
**Style:** "Mixing high low really well." Smart shoes with shorts and sports socks.
**Key brands:** Ghiaia Cashmere, Rubato, L.E.J., The Anthology, Brycelands, AWMS.
**Quotes:** "All this selection means guys can take a lot more risks, style wise."
**Insight:** A seller who curates decisively around one coherent aesthetic cuts through noise better than a broad generalist.

### Kenneth MacKenzie — 6876
**Profession:** Designer and founder of cult menswear brand 6876 (since 1995). Based at Brunswick Centre, London.
**Style:** Anti-fashion, anti-season. Modernist design. Influenced by May 1968, punk 1976, military clothing, brutalist architecture.
**Key brands:** 6876 (own label). Collaborations with Clarks, Fred Perry, Arpenteur.
**Quotes:** "6876 has always been about the product first, above marketing and hype."
**Insight:** Deadstock from an independent designer is archive access — an entirely different proposition to pre-owned from a wardrobe. Cultivate designer relationships.

### Richard Wharton — Office / Offspring Founder
**Profession:** Footwear retail pioneer. Co-founded Office (1983), Offspring (1995), Poste and Poste Mistress.
**Style:** Rare sneaker collector. Sources globally, especially Japan. Also appreciates Paraboot, Trickers, Buttero.
**Key brands:** New Balance 576 (colorways he personally designed), Paraboot, Trickers, Buttero.
**Insight:** Provenance matters enormously. A single-run collab becomes a cultural document. Knowing and telling that story separates a seller from a shopkeeper.

### Karlmond Tang — Stylist
**Profession:** London-based stylist and art director. Styled for Daniel Arsham's Objects IV Life, A-Cold-Wall*.
**Style:** Structured and analytical. Films as reference.
**Key brands:** Issey Miyake/Homme Plisse, Armani (80s/90s), Comme des Garcons, Drake's, Our Legacy, Lemaire, Jil Sander.
**Quotes:** "I am constantly buying second-hand clothing. I am a firm believer in re-selling." / "I'd recommend only buying one or two items of the same type."
**Insight:** Versatility as a selling argument — demonstrating the multiple contexts a single piece can serve addresses the "I don't have an occasion for it" objection.

### Neil Bedford & Ben Benoliel — The Artists Pad
**Profession:** Northern photographers. Co-founded creative retreat in Saltburn, North Yorkshire. Neil has shot Liam Gallagher, collaborated with Nigel Cabourn. Ben co-founded LN-CC.
**Style:** Accumulative and personal rather than brand-led.
**Insight:** A shared seller identity can be stronger than solo. Creative backstory adds narrative weight — buyers feel they're getting a piece of a creative life.

### Aleks Cvetkovic — FT / Monocle Journalist
**Profession:** Journalist covering men's luxury for FT and Monocle. Founder of Standfirst Creative Strategy. Former host of Handcut Radio.
**Style:** Restrained colour palettes, high craft, contemporary modular design.
**Key brands:** Stoffa, Saman Amel, The Row.
**Quotes:** "Consumers need to change their priorities and brands need to revisit their responsibility to consumers and to the planet."
**Insight:** Values-driven buyers want to feel part of a conscious shift, not just a transaction.

### Adam Luck — Abbot's Shoes
**Profession:** Founder of Abbot's, a pre-owned footwear platform. Former financial services. Self-taught shoe restoration.
**Style:** Classic English Goodyear welt footwear.
**Key brands:** Church's, Private White VC, Tricker's, Edward Green, Crockett & Jones.
**Quotes:** "Leather shoes can take quite a beating but with the correct care can last for decades."
**Insight:** Goodyear welt construction is the key quality signal for footwear. Restored pre-owned shoes can reach near-new condition.

### David Hellqvist — Document Studios
**Profession:** Writer, stylist, art director. Swedish, London-based. Former Dazed Digital editor. Founder of Document Studios. Career started in retail at Browns and Dover Street Market.
**Style:** "Functional realism as opposed to dreams of decadence."
**Key brands:** Drake's, Visvim, Gitman Vintage, CP Company, Universal Works.
**Quotes:** "My outlook on menswear is defined by functional realism as opposed to dreams of decadence."
**Insight:** Retail experience builds an eye that no amount of online browsing replaces — understanding what a garment actually is in the hand is foundational.

### Anthony Lee — Photographer
**Profession:** Photographer and content creator, Liverpool. Primary photographer for This Thing Of Ours.
**Style:** Technical/functional materials, relaxed fit, subtle branding. Anti-trend.
**Key brands:** Nanamica, Our Legacy, New Balance 990v3, Salomon, Daiwa Pier39.
**Quotes:** "I invest in pieces that aren't trend driven and that will work within my wardrobe for the foreseeable."
**Insight:** Buyers in technical menswear know specific model numbers. Accurate, specific descriptions ("990v3" not just "New Balance") signal credibility.

### Fei Wang — Mr Slowboy
**Profession:** Fashion illustrator. Beijing-born, London-based. Former advertising creative director. Published "Mr Slowboy: Portraits of the Modern Gentleman."
**Style:** Sartorial, gentleman-leaning with Japanese menswear influence. Inspired by Kazuo Hozumi's "Ivy Boys."
**Key brands:** Drake's ("all-time favourite brand"), Post Overalls, Old Joe Brand.
**Insight:** The menswear illustration community has significant influence on how buyers visualise and romanticise certain garments. This audience responds strongly to story-driven presentation.

### Christopher Moorby — Commission Studio
**Profession:** Creative Director and co-founder of Commission Studio (branding agency). Clients include Rimowa and Zegna.
**Style:** Made-in provenance obsessive. Made in USA Ralph Lauren, Made in France Moncler, Made in Japan brands.
**Key brands:** The Real McCoy's, Drake's, Jelado, Pherrows, Warehouse, Full Count, Ralph Lauren (MiUSA), The North Face, Moncler (MiF).
**Quotes:** "It feels like selling through Marrkt, my clothes will end up with someone who has a similar appreciation for them as I did."
**Insight:** Country-of-manufacture and production era are significant value signals. Always research and state manufacturing origin in listings.

### Khabi Mirza — Fabric PR
**Profession:** Co-founder of Fabric PR. 30 years in journalism (Metal Hammer, GQ, Menswear magazine, Drapers).
**Style:** "Thirst for colour, pattern and texture." Mixes genres freely. Self-described "hopeless hoarder."
**Key brands:** Visvim, Engineered Garments, Monitaly, Incotex, Patagonia, Arc'teryx.
**Insight:** Buyers who've been in menswear for decades have passed through multiple eras. A seller who can articulate the cultural moment a piece belongs to will connect better.

**Style:** East London, modern architecture, cycling, running intersecting with menswear.

### Matthew Spade — Buckets & Spades
**Profession:** Content creator and menswear influencer, Blackpool. Blog "Buckets & Spades" since 2008. Also runs @bestofpackaging. Sells art prints globally.
**Style:** Traditional sportswear, vintage American cinema-inspired. Relaxed colour mixing. Blues, navy, denim core.
**Key brands:** Levi's, Another Aspect, Drake's, Barbour, Universal Works, Albam, And Wander.

### Kevin Kafesu — Norse Projects
**Profession:** Head of Buying and Communication at Norse Projects, Copenhagen. Previously at Andersen-Andersen and Wood Wood. Co-founded fashion blog arewu.
**Style:** Japanese labels, quality basics, pieces that age well.
**Key brands:** ts(s), Visvim, Veilance, Frame, Needles.
**Quotes:** "The role of the buyer doesn't finish at the end of buying appointments" — extends through the entire customer journey.
**Insight:** Industry professionals (buyers, designers) are both sellers AND buyers of pre-owned. Their pieces carry professional credibility and are highly curated.

### Chris Kontos — Kennedy Magazine
**Profession:** Photographer and editor of Kennedy Magazine (independent long-form culture publication). Based in Basel, Switzerland.
**Style:** Minimalist quality over quantity. Japanese brands and American-made basics. Audio equipment enthusiast.
**Key brands:** Gitman Vintage, Engineered Garments, Our Legacy, Orslow, Resolute, Camoshita.
**Quotes:** "People buy too much. I invest in quality always." / "I'm always on the lookout for used stuff that is good shape and will be ready to serve someone for another few good years."
**Insight:** The quality-over-quantity buyer is actively looking for pre-owned — they're not settling, they're choosing. This reframes second-hand as a deliberate quality choice.

### Thom Barnett — Mamnick
**Profession:** Sheffield-based designer. Runs Mamnick for 10+ years — locally manufactured in South Yorkshire. Produces angling jumpers, cycling kit, parkas. Accomplished cyclist.
**Style:** Authenticity, craftsmanship, local production. Working-class background — vintage shopping was how he accessed quality affordably.
**Key brands:** Nigel Cabourn, 6876, Engineered Garments (sells pre-owned). Mamnick (own label).
**Quotes:** "One thing at a time, as beautiful as possible."
**Insight:** The working-class-to-quality pipeline is a powerful origin story for a vintage seller. Vintage was the affordable entry point to quality brands — that's a narrative many buyers relate to.
