## Tone spectrum

```
Formal ●●●●○ Casual
Reserved ●●●○○ Expressive
Understated ●●●●○ Bold
Expert ●●●●○ Accessible
```

The voice is **premium but warm** — closer to a private bank than a tech startup, but never cold or corporate. Think Belmond, not Booking.com.

## Do

- Use "we" and "your" — personal, direct
- Lead with the human outcome, not the service feature
- Reference longevity and experience naturally (not as a boast)
- Use specific hospitality language (GM, F&B, executive housekeeper) — the audience knows it
- Keep sentences short and declarative
- Use the word "discreet" over "confidential" where possible (softer, more premium)

## Don't

- Use "headhunters" in premium contexts (acceptable in SEO titles, not in brand copy)
- Use "#1", "best", "leading" — superlatives undercut the understated positioning
- Use "free" on UHNW-facing pages (price sensitivity signal)
- Use "Learn more" as a CTA — it says nothing
- Use passive voice for service descriptions ("candidates are matched" → "we match candidates")
- Use tech jargon (API, CRM, ATS) in client-facing copy
- Use "recently", "currently", "new" without a date (freshness rot)

## Vocabulary preferences

| Prefer | Avoid |
|--------|-------|
| Discreet | Confidential (in headlines) |
| Place / connect | Match / fill |
| Hospitality professionals | Candidates (in brand copy) |
| Executive search | Headhunting (in brand copy) |
| Private consultation | Free proposal |
| Tailored | Customised |
| Since 1999 | 25+ years (use the date, it's more authoritative) |

## Voice by audience

| Audience | Tone adjustment |
|----------|----------------|
| **Candidates** | Warmer, more personal. "Your career is a journey." Aspirational but grounded. |
| **Employers** | More direct, results-oriented. "We deliver talent." Evidence-backed. |
| **UHNW / Private** | Most reserved and premium. "Exceptional recruitment for the world's most discerning households." Zero superlatives. |
| **Blog / Insights** | Slightly more casual, thought-leadership tone. Expert sharing knowledge, not selling. |
