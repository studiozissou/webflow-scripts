# Headline Writing — Notes

## Suggested Headlines for TSC

Approach: benefit leads, keyword is the object. "Do [benefit] with [keyword]" pattern. Where the keyword genuinely can't fit the H1 without killing readability, it drops to the sub and is flagged.

**Home**
SEO keyword: ETCS onboard system
H1: Safety-critical signalling, software-defined
Sub: The ETCS onboard system built on an open platform. Lighter hardware, lower lifetime cost, zero obsolescence risk.
Keyword in H1: No — homepage needs to set tone, not target a single keyword. "ETCS onboard system" as an H1 undersells the company. Keyword anchored in sub.

**RailOS**
SEO keyword: software-defined railway signalling
H1: One platform. Every ATP system.
Sub: RailOS pioneered software-defined railway signalling — portable, scalable, and open to third-party development.
Keyword in H1: No — "software-defined railway signalling" is a 4-word phrase that can't be shortened. Forcing it in loses the punch. Keyword in sub.

## Summary

9 out of 13 have the keyword in the H1. The 4 exceptions:
- **Home** — needs to set brand tone, not target a product keyword
- **RailOS** — "software-defined railway signalling" is too long a phrase to fit naturally
- **Customers** — "ETCS case study" doesn't work as a customer-facing headline
- **About** — "railway signalling company" is generic and loses the challenger story

All 4 carry the keyword prominently in the subheading instead.

## Principles

1. **Benefit leads, keyword follows.** The headline sells; the keyword tells Google what the page is about.
2. **Keep it under 10 words.** Detail goes in the subheading.
3. **Avoid internal terminology in headlines.** "iEVC" and "wSTM" belong in body copy.
4. **7-second test.** Headline + subheading should tell a visitor what the page is about and whether it's for them.
5. **Keyword in the H1 where it fits naturally.** If forcing it kills the benefit or the rhythm, drop it to the subheading — but make sure it appears in the first line of visible text.
