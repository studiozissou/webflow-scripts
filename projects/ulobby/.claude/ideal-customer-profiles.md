# Ulobby — Ideal Customer Profiles

**Date:** 2026-06-08
**Source:** Copy analysis across homepage, about/story, solutions, plans, blog, and pricing tiers.

## ICP 1: The In-House PA Team Lead

### Role / Demographic
Head of Public Affairs, VP Government Relations, or Senior PA Manager at a mid-to-large organisation (100-5,000 employees). Industry sectors: energy, pharma, tech, financial services, trade associations. Based in the EU, Nordics, or Brussels corridor. Manages a team of 2-8 PA professionals. Reports to the C-suite and needs to justify the function's value internally.

### Goals
- Build a structured, repeatable PA workflow that survives staff turnover
- Track stakeholder relationships and issue developments in one place instead of across inboxes, spreadsheets, and personal notes
- Produce clear reports for senior leadership that show what the PA function accomplished and why it mattered
- Catch political developments early enough to influence outcomes rather than react to them
- Professionalise the PA function so it is treated as a strategic discipline, not a reactive support role

### Pain Points
- Critical institutional knowledge lives in individuals' heads and leaves when they do
- No single system of record for stakeholder interactions — fragmented across Outlook, Excel, CRM workarounds, shared drives
- Reporting to management is manual, time-consuming, and always undersells the team's work
- Hard to demonstrate ROI of Public Affairs — activity metrics don't capture strategic influence
- Political monitoring is ad hoc: no one has the full picture
- Colleagues duplicate effort or miss follow-ups because there is no shared visibility

### Language They Use
- "Stakeholder engagement", "issue monitoring", "political intelligence"
- "We need oversight across the team"
- "How do we keep institutional memory when people leave?"
- "I need to show the board what we actually do"
- "We're always in firefighting mode"
- Search terms: "Public Affairs software", "stakeholder management platform", "political monitoring tool EU"

### What They Need From the Site
- Immediate recognition that this is built for PA (not a repurposed CRM)
- Evidence that Ulobby understands the discipline's unique challenges
- Demonstration of reporting capabilities that translate PA activity into board-level insight
- Case studies or social proof from comparable organisations
- Enterprise-grade security and SSO for IT procurement approval
- A demo path that respects their time

### Plan Fit
Professional or Enterprise.

## ICP 2: The Solo PA Practitioner or Small Consultancy

### Role / Demographic
Independent PA consultant or small consultancy (1-3 people) advising clients on political strategy, regulatory risk, and stakeholder engagement. Based in Copenhagen, Brussels, Oslo, Stockholm, or other Nordic/EU capitals. Juggles multiple client mandates simultaneously.

### Goals
- Monitor political developments across multiple issues and jurisdictions efficiently
- Maintain a structured record of stakeholder interactions across client mandates
- Deliver professional reports to clients without hours of manual compilation
- Stay ahead of regulatory changes

### Pain Points
- Information overload: too many political sources, not enough time to synthesise
- Context switching between clients means details get lost
- No budget for enterprise tools, but spreadsheets are not professional enough
- Producing structured intelligence is the most time-consuming part of the job
- Works alone — everything depends on one person staying on top of it

### Language They Use
- "I need to keep track of everything"
- "My clients expect me to know what's happening before they do"
- "Issue tracking", "political landscape", "dossier"
- Search terms: "political monitoring software", "stakeholder management for consultants", "EU legislative tracking tool"

### What They Need From the Site
- Starter plan clearly positioned for individuals (not a stripped-down version)
- Proof a single person can get value without team workflows
- Clear pricing or tier positioning before committing to a demo call
- Fast onboarding — no multi-week implementation projects

### Plan Fit
Starter.

## ICP 3: The Policy Director (Enterprise)

### Role / Demographic
Director of Government Affairs, Chief Policy Officer, or equivalent at a large corporation, industry federation, or multinational NGO. Team of 8-25+ PA professionals across multiple offices (Brussels + national capitals). Manages PA across multiple markets and legislative bodies.

### Goals
- Enterprise-wide visibility into what every team member is tracking across jurisdictions
- Governance and compliance around lobbying activities (transparency registers)
- Standardised reporting that rolls up from analysts to team leads to the board
- Break down silos between Brussels, national offices, and sector teams
- Demonstrate strategic value of the PA function with data, not anecdotes

### Pain Points
- No single source of truth across offices — Brussels tracks MEPs, national offices track their parliaments, none share a system
- Compliance risk: transparency requirements differ by jurisdiction, ad hoc tracking creates audit vulnerability
- Senior leadership doesn't understand what PA does because reporting is inconsistent
- Onboarding new team members takes months because institutional knowledge is undocumented
- IT procurement requirements (SSO, data residency, security certs) eliminate smaller tools

### Language They Use
- "Cross-jurisdictional", "multi-market", "enterprise-grade"
- "Single source of truth"
- "Compliance", "transparency register", "audit trail"
- "Governance", "oversight", "accountability"
- Search terms: "enterprise Public Affairs platform", "lobbying compliance software EU", "PA software with SSO"

### What They Need From the Site
- Enterprise tier with explicit SSO, security, data handling, deployment support
- Evidence of multi-market, multi-language capability
- Procurement-friendly path: dedicated contact, security docs, SLA info
- Content that speaks to strategic PA leadership, not just practitioners
- Case studies or logos from comparable large organisations

### Plan Fit
Enterprise.

## Cross-Cutting Observations

**What all ICPs share:**
- Deep domain expertise in PA — they don't need the concept explained
- Frustration with fragmented, manual workflows
- Need to make their work visible and defensible to non-PA leadership
- European operating context with multi-jurisdictional complexity
- Preference for professional, understated communication — they distrust hype

**Gaps in current copy:**
- **ICP 1 is best-served.** Homepage, solutions, and blog all speak to the team-lead PA professional.
- **ICP 2 is underserved.** Starter tier exists but no copy addresses the solo practitioner's challenges. Blog's team framing may feel exclusionary.
- **ICP 3 is implied but underdeveloped.** Security, compliance, multi-market deployment mentioned as bullets but not developed into a narrative.
- **Politicians as audience:** The stated target includes "politicians and EU decision-makers" but the entire site speaks to PA professionals. If politicians are genuinely a target, they need their own entry point.
- **Social proof gap:** Client logos, case studies, and named testimonials are absent or not prominent. Significant gap for risk-averse enterprise buyers.
