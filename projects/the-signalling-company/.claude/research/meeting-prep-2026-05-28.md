## Block 3 — Template review + choices

### Shortlisted templates

**1. Reforma ($129)** — Top pick
https://webflow.com/templates/html/reforma-website-template
- Client-First naming + BYQ Framework — cleanest class system, easiest to maintain
- CMS for services, case studies, blog — maps directly to TSC's sitemap
- GSAP animations, cookie banner built in
- "Calm, structured, credible" — matches TSC's "plain apron" philosophy
- 3 homepage, 3 about, 3 contact page variations
- Careers page included
- Gap: no gated download pattern out of the box (easy to build)

**2. Cargo+ ($129)** — Strong structure, logistics-adjacent
https://webflow.com/templates/html/cargo-website-template
- BYQ Framework 4.0 — clean class system (not Client-First but well-structured)
- Light/dark mode, Figma file, variable-driven design
- 40+ modular sections, CMS for case studies and news
- Multiple homepage and about page variations
- Risk: logistics/freight branding baked in — needs more reskinning

**3. Halden Miller ($129)** — Clean B2B consulting feel
https://webflow.com/templates/html/halden-miller-website-template
- BYQ Framework 4.0 — same clean class system as Cargo+
- GSAP animations, light/dark mode, Figma file
- Case studies, blog, book-a-call modal — good B2B structure
- Trust-focused design with 40+ modular sections
- Risk: designed for consulting/services, not product companies — needs adaptation to show products

**Backup: Clarity Catalyst ($59)** — Client-First, budget option
https://webflow.com/templates/html/clarity-catalyst-website-template
- Client-First naming, GSAP animations, 13 pages
- Cheapest option but less modular — coaching/consulting framing needs reskinning
- No Figma file or design tokens. Simpler structure overall.

### Questions to ask
- Show the three shortlisted templates. For each: what works out of the box, what needs adaptation, what's a limitation.
- Set expectations: the template sets the layout and interaction tone. You adapt colour, type, spacing, imagery — you don't redesign sections.
- How does their colour identity map to the template? Discuss where accent colours appear (section backgrounds, icons, CTAs).
- Confirm: this is a final or near-final choice. Changing template later resets the clock.

### Watch for
- If they want heavy layout changes, that's custom work. Be clear about where "adaptation" ends and "redesign" begins.

## Block 4 — Technical questions

### Content prep
- Who delivers content, in what format, and to where? (Google Doc, Notion, email?)
- Image specs: do they know what resolutions/aspect ratios are needed? Offer to send a brief.

### Form walls / gated downloads
- Confirm: Webflow native forms, no CRM integration in phase 1.
- What happens with form submissions? Just Webflow export, or do they need a Zapier/email hook?
- Odoo CRM: confirm this is phase 2. What are they doing with leads in the meantime?

### URL history / SEO
- Do they have an existing site with indexed URLs? If so, need a redirect map.
- Any existing Google Search Console or Analytics they can give you access to?
- Domain and DNS: who manages this? Any existing email (MX records) that could conflict?
- Are there specific keywords or search terms they care about ranking for?

### GDPR
- Cookie consent: confirm they understand the $199 plugin cost (CookieYes or similar).
- Privacy policy: do they have one, or does it need writing? (Not in your scope.)
- Any specific data processing requirements from Belgian/EU law they're aware of?

### Hosting and handover
- Webflow hosting: $300/year paid to Webflow. Are they ok with this?
- Do they have a Webflow account, or do you need to set one up?
- Post-launch: gauge interest in a monthly retainer vs one-and-done.
