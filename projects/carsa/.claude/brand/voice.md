# Brand Voice -- Carsa

## Tone
Professional but approachable. Carsa is a used car dealership based in
Middlesbrough serving the Teesside and North East England market. The voice
should feel like talking to a knowledgeable friend who happens to sell cars --
not a salesperson reading a script.

## Principles

1. **Straightforward.** Say what you mean. No filler, no fluff. If the car has
   60,000 miles on it, say so plainly.
2. **Honest.** Transparency builds trust. Clear pricing, upfront descriptions,
   no hidden conditions.
3. **Accessible.** Write for car buyers, not the motor trade. Avoid industry
   jargon unless it genuinely helps the reader (and define it if you use it).
4. **Warm but not matey.** Friendly without being overfamiliar. This is a
   significant purchase -- respect that.

## Always use
- Clear pricing and finance terms
- Transparent vehicle descriptions
- Straightforward CTAs ("View this car", "Get a finance quote", "Book a test drive")
- Plain language explanations of finance, warranty, and part-exchange processes
- Active voice

## Never use
- "Best deals", "unbeatable", "incredible value", "bargain"
- "Limited time only", "act now", "don't miss out"
- Exclamation marks in headings
- Pushy sales language or artificial urgency
- Superlatives without evidence ("the best", "number one")
- "We pride ourselves" or similar corporate cliches
- Jargon without explanation (CAP clean, book price, etc.)

## CTA patterns
- Primary: "Search cars", "Get a finance quote", "Book a test drive"
- Secondary: "Learn more", "See how it works", "Find your nearest store"
- Avoid: "Buy now", "Get yours today", "Grab this deal"
