# Carsa — Brand Voice

## Tone Adjectives

1. **Straightforward** — short declarative statements, no fluff
2. **Reassuring** — emphasises zero-risk, refundable, no-obligation at every step
3. **Modern** — tech-forward language ("omni-channel", "personalised", "in 60 seconds")
4. **Friendly** — approachable without being overly casual ("the car's all yours", "keep your journeys stress-free")
5. **Confident** — makes bold claims backed by specifics ("Save £700 on average", "2,000+ vehicles")

## Do Words / Phrases

- simple, made simple
- free, no obligation, no impact on your credit score
- personalised
- flexible
- transparent
- checked, verified, prepared
- peace of mind
- in minutes / in seconds / instant
- stress-free
- trusted
- ready to go

## Don't Words / Phrases

- bargain, cheap, budget (they say "affordable" or "save")
- hassle (they imply hassle-free without using the cliche)
- deal of a lifetime, once-in-a-lifetime
- used car dealer (they say "car retailer")
- salespeople, salesman
- complicated, confusing
- gamble, risk
- jargon-heavy finance terms without explanation
- pushy urgency language ("hurry", "last chance", "don't miss out")

## Vocabulary Level

**Casual-professional.** Plain English aimed at consumers who may not know PCP from HP. Technical terms (APR, PCP, HP) are always followed by a one-sentence explanation in everyday language. Reading level sits around UK tabloid / broadsheet crossover — accessible but not dumbed down.

## Sentence Style

- Headlines: 2-6 words, fragment or short sentence ("Car finance. Made simple.")
- Subheadings: question form or short benefit statement
- Body copy: 1-2 sentences per paragraph, max ~25 words per sentence
- Heavy use of em-dashes for asides rather than parentheses
- Lists over paragraphs for features/benefits
- Numbers always specific (£700, 90 days, 8.9%, 60 seconds)
