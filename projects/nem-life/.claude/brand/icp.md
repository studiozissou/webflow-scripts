# Ideal Customer Profile — NEM Life

## Segment: "Anna" (Primary)
- **Demographics:** Women 35-55, emotionally intelligent, Dutch-speaking, middle-to-upper income
- **Goals:** Break recurring relationship/behaviour patterns; find clarity without years of traditional therapy; feel understood without being pathologised
- **Pain points:** Stuck in loops (same arguments, same emotional reactions); exhausted by "just talking about it"; overwhelmed by advice that doesn't stick; feels like something is wrong with her
- **Buying triggers:** A moment of recognition ("this is me"); seeing the 3-step methodology as structured and achievable; Christel's personal story creating trust; testimonials from women like her
- **Objections:** "Is this just another coaching programme?"; "I've tried therapy before"; "Can I do this alongside my busy life?"; cost sensitivity (therapy is expensive)
- **Messaging angle:** "You're not the problem — you just need a clear approach." Lead with recognition of the pattern, then show the structured path out. Never position as therapy replacement; position as the missing structure.

## Segment: Men (Secondary)
- **Demographics:** Men 35-55, reflective, often triggered by relationship crisis or partner's suggestion
- **Goals:** Understand their own patterns; improve relationship dynamics; find practical tools (not "feelings talk")
- **Pain points:** Traditional therapy feels too slow or too emotional; wants concrete steps; may feel stigma around seeking help
- **Buying triggers:** Partner's recommendation; seeing the methodology as structured/logical; "without endless talking" resonates
- **Objections:** Stigma; "this isn't for men"; time commitment
- **Messaging angle:** Lead with the practical, structured nature. "An approach that works" over "emotional healing." The NEM Method's three pillars (Neuro, Emotional, Mastery) frame it as a skill to develop, not a wound to heal.
