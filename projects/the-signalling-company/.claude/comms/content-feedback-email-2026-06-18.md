# Email to Romain — Content Feedback

**Subject:** Copy feedback — 3 things to focus on

---

Hi Romain,

Thanks for your call today. I've been through the content doc. I think the depth and detail on the product is great. Here are the three things I'd focus on to make the copy work harder to attact visitors on the web:

**1. Lead with the customer benefit, not the technical description.**
Most pages currently open with what TSC built: the architecture, the components, the platform. On the web, visitors decide in about 7 seconds whether a page is for them, so each page needs a short, benefit-led headline under 10 words and a 1-2 sentence subheading that answers "what does this mean for me?" before getting into the detail.

**2. Differentiate each customer segment.**
The Customers page currently uses the same Why/How/What copy across all four segments, with only the audience name swapped out. I would suggest 2-3 tailored sentences per segment to make each one feel like it's speaking directly to that buyer. E.g. A fleet operator cares about minimising downtime during retrofit. A leasing company cares about protecting asset value over a 20-year lifecycle. Etc.

**3. Surface the proof points because they're your strongest content.**
TSC has genuinely trustworthy credentials: 109 locomotives for Lineas, the Skoda 27Ev project, the RailTech Innovation Award, SIL4 certification, Skoda Group ownership. Right now, these are buried in paragraphs. Pull them out as standalone stats, add them to the homepage as a trust bar, and structure the case studies with clear challenge/solution/result sections. Proof is key for safety-conscious buyers.

I'll add specific inline comments to the doc as well.
