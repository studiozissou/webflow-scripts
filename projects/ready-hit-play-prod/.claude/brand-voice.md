# Ready Hit Play — Brand Voice

## Tone Adjectives

1. **Commanding** — short declarative statements that brook no argument
2. **Visceral** — appeals to physical sensation over intellect ("goosebumps don't lie")
3. **Spare** — says less, means more; whitespace is part of the voice
4. **Cinematic** — writes in scenes and rooms, not features and benefits
5. **Assured** — never hedges, never qualifies, never apologizes

## Do Words

- undeniable, inevitable, impact, story, room, rise, move, moment
- built, made, stand, step, feel, change, matter
- connection, intention, people, great, first

## Don't Words

- innovative, synergy, leverage, utilize, solutions, cutting-edge
- passionate, world-class, best-in-class, disrupt
- excited, thrilled, proud (emotional self-reporting)
- click here, learn more, submit (generic CTAs)
- recently, new, this year (time-sensitive hedges)

## Vocabulary Level

**Professional-literary.** Not academic, not casual. Closer to a film voiceover than a pitch deck. No jargon. No acronyms. Every word earns its place.

## Sentence Style

- Fragments are welcome. Short sentences dominate (5–12 words).
- Parallel structure and repetition are signature devices: "The scale changes. The intention never does." / "The room changes. The standard doesn't."
- One idea per sentence. One sentence per beat.
- Headlines often land as 3–5 word statements.
- Body copy uses rhetorical contrast: "Reach asks X. Impact asks Y."
- No em-dashes, no parentheticals, minimal commas.

## Sample Phrases That Capture the Voice

- "Goosebumps don't lie."
- "Great stories made undeniable."
- "Most creative work is made for reach. Ours is made for impact. There's a difference."
- "Reach asks how many people will see it. Impact asks what will happen to the people who do."
- "The room would rise."
- "The story escapes."
- "It starts with a conversation."
- "Language that makes you impossible to ignore. Built to travel."
- "Step in. Stand out."
